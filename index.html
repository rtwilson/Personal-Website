Work Experience

During the spring and summer of 2012, I completed an internship at Nicira (recently acquired by VMware) working in the Tools team. The Tools team creates, optimizes and repairs build tools, which involves a combination of programming and system administrator work. My primary project was to design a distributed compilation system to improve build times on the second phase of the process known as the “second make”. Not only was it critical that the system reduce build time, it was imperative that the system be resistant to failure. After completing this task, the “second make”, once requiring 130 minutes, now completes in 40 minutes, a 70% improvement. Furthermore, I created a web application using the Django framework to aggregate and visualize build information. I also added automated bug finding using git bisect to Nicira's unit testing software.

Research

During the summer of 2012, I worked with Stanford’s computer science research group MobiSocial to develop a mobile music application for iPhone, known as Jinzora. I implemented remote downloads and local caching features and integrated Jinzora with the MobiSocial's social application Musubi for Iphone to enable playlist sharing between friends.

During the summer of 2011, I conducted robotics research at UCLA with a team of students from Harvey Mudd College under the guidance of Professor Jerome Gilles. Using a Xilinx FPGA console and Matlab, we made significant progress toward implementing robot swarm-to-swarm communication in real time with another robotics team at the University of Cincinnati. While communication between the two robot swarms was complicated by dissimilar API's, we were able to perform simulations between two virtual swarms over a network and measure the performance. We also achieved real-time swarm-to-swarm communication between a swarm of actual robots and a virtual swarm. In particular, I wrote the code for the swarming algorithm on both the simulated and actual robots, the network communication scripts and the data parsing functions. I also designed a barrier avoidance algorithm, which was successful both in simulation and implementation. We published a paper entitled "Robot Swarming Over the Internet," which was submitted to the 2012 American Control Conference.

During the summer of 2010, I was among a team of students at UCLA doing research in mathematical biology, led by Professor Maria-Grazia-Ascenzi and Professor George Mohler. The goals of this project were to mathematically model cell reproduction in the growth plate and statistically analyze cell photographs to confirm the accuracy of these models. My assigned task in this project was to use image processing and statistical techniques to accelerate data collection, which, before this point, was done manually. The paper is published in Volume 285, Issue 1 of the Journal of Theoretical Biology as "Effect of localization, length and orientation of chondrocytic primary cilium on murine growth plate organization."
